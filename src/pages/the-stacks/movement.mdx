The work is not optional. Compounds and tracking amplify training — they don't
replace it. This pillar is the actual training life: what a week really looks
like when two people with full schedules refuse to skip it.

## What lives here

- **Workout logs** — form, weight, and progress over time. The numbers, not the vibes.
- **Ruck POVs** — SoCal outdoor training, Louie in frame.
- **Lifting** — programming that respects recovery in midlife instead of pretending we're 25.
- **Hot yoga** — the recap format: what changed, what it cost, whether it earned its slot.
- **HIIT and conditioning** — short, hard, scheduled.
- **Recovery** — what rest days actually look like, and the mobility work that keeps the rest possible.
- **Travel fitness** — hotel workouts and keeping the routine on the road.
- **Home-gym training** — for people without a full facility.

## The frame

A training week here is built around consistency over intensity theater. We
batch what can be batched, log what can be measured, and show the plateaus
alongside the progress. If a session gets skipped, the log says so.
