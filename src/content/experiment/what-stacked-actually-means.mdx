People hear "stacked" and assume it's about the physique. Fair — that's one
meaning. But it's the last one you earn, not the first one you choose.

Here's the architecture underneath it.

## The four stacks

**Optimization.** The data layer. What we run, what we measure, and what the
labs say. Wearables read the outside, blood work reads the inside, and every
addition to the stack has to move a number or lose its seat.

**Movement.** The training. Lifting, conditioning, rucking, the mobility work
that keeps all of it possible. Nothing else in the system works if this is
missing — compounds amplify training, they don't replace it.

**Fuel.** The nutrition framework. Protein and hydration as non-negotiables,
macro targets that fit the goal, and a framework two people with different goals
can share.

**Mind.** Sleep, focus, stress load — and the honest conversation about what
midlife actually looks like from inside it. This one makes or breaks the other
three.

## The fifth meaning

Stacked is also two people holding each other to the same standard — different
goals, shared discipline. One person can run a protocol. Two people can run an
experiment, keep each other honest, and catch what the other one misses.

That's the meaning that makes the rest sustainable.
